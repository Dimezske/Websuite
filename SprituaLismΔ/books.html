Book of Shadows

A Book of Shadows is a book comprising of religious text and instructions for magical rituals and information about deities found within the Neopagan religion of Wicca. Since its perception in the 1970s, it has made its way into various pagan practices and paths. it is the most famous Book of Shadows created by the pioneering Wiccan Gerald Gardner associated with wiccan magick within in 1950s, and which he utilised first in his Bricket Wood coven and then in other covens which he founded in following decades. The Book of Shadows is also used by other Wiccan traditions, such as Alexandrian Wicca and Mohsianism, and with the rise of books teaching people how to begin following non-initiatory Wicca in the 1970s onward, the idea of the Book of Shadows was then further propagated amongst solitary practitioners unconnected to earlier, initiatory traditions. Gerald Gardner, the "father of Wicca", first introduced the Book of Shadows to people that he had initiated into the craft through his Bricket Wood coven in the 1950s A leather bound manuscript written in Gardner's handwriting that was titled Ye Bok of Ye Art Magical was later found amongst his papers from the Museum after his death by Aidan Kelly and was later obtained by Richard and Tamarra James of the Wiccan Church of Canada, Initially when Wicca was still dominated by covens, "only one copy of the Book existed for an entire coven, kept by the high priestess or high priest.
